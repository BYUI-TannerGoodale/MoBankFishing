Recommended Gear

These are the setups I would recommend for new and experienced river bank fishers alike. I'll have a brief description of each item and my recommendations for paired gear. All links are affiliate links.

Rods

I've always been fond of spinning gear myself and recommend beginners to start with it. Each rod will be made for either spinning or casting reels. It is important to pair the appropriate rod with it's corresponding reel to have proper operation of both. Spinning reels are ideal for bank fishing on big rivers and have been gaining in popularity over the past few years. A spinning reel faces downward and sits naturally in-between an anglers fingers when holding the rod. Spinning reels also come in a variety of sizes and the largest of them have copious line capacity for big river bank fishing. They also generally allow for further casting then other types of reels. It's for these reasons that I prefer them and will show you my favorite options here. Remember though, a rod and a reel are only a part of the equation. I'll also have links to tackle that I recommend.

Hooks

Circle hooks are king on the river. They are self-setting hooks that hold bait very well and are designed to hook a fish's lips. This is great for catch and release fishing because the fish is almost never fatally harmed by the hook set. These hooks are also used by food fishermen because they have a great hookup ratio and fish almost never come off if properly hooked. One of the biggest lessons I've learned about hooks, you get what you pay for. They don't cost a ton, but you'll be sad if you use the cheap stuff.

Old and reliable

Gamakatsu Octopus Circle Hooks


Purchase on Amazon

Gamakatsu is a quality brand trusted by fishermen everywhere. Their 8/0 circle hooks have never failed me and have proven to be a high quality option. The hooks are made from high quality steel and hold a point better then any of the budget options available.

Though I couldn't find any links on Amazon, another hook I have come to trust and love is the Team Catfish 8/0 circle hooks sold at Walmart. These hooks are high quality and very strong. I've never had any issues with them and their bent eye makes tying a snell knot easy.

Cast net

A cast net is one of the most important things a catfish angler needs in their inventory. A cast net allows you to get bait with a throw of the arm, though they take a bit of practice to get used to and cast well. I've personally never needed one larger then 3 1/2' wide, but there are times where one a little larger would have been helpful. Anything larger then 5' wide can be a bit unwieldy from the bank, so I wouldn't go much further then that. Walmart sells cast nets in certain areas and some can be found on amazon, though I have never used any of the ones from amazon myself.

I can't speak for the quality of the net myself, but the reviews were very good and the price is right for high quality. Best Fisher's is a name I've seen used by many other river anglers as well.

Outside of the gear shown here, other things you'll want to get include barrel swivels, lead sliding sinkers and sinker stops. I'll be sure to add links to good examples later on. For the swivels, go for 1/0 size. For the weights, egg or no-roll sinkers are a good choice ranging from 4-8oz. Be sure to get a variety between those sizes. I'm fond of rubber sinker stops. Be sure to put them in front of your weight so it doesn't damage your swivel knots.
